LUXBIN

Quantum-Classical Hybrid Cryptography with Acoustic Shielding and LDD Consensus

By Nichole Christie
Independent Researcher

Abstract

LUXBIN introduces a novel quantum-classical hybrid cryptographic system combining three innovative components: Acoustic Quantum Shielding for environmental noise control, LDD (Lightning Diamond Device) Consensus for scalable blockchain validation, and Trinity Cryptography for hardware-bound key generation. This research presents experimental validation of all components using cloud GPU infrastructure and quantum simulation, demonstrating acoustic wave interference patterns, consensus scaling to 50,000+ validators, cryptographic key uniqueness, and quantum circuit integration via Google's Cirq framework. The system achieves 512-bit security strength through multi-factor key generation combining hardware physics, acoustic environments, temporal constraints, and quantum-compatible cryptographic primitives.
